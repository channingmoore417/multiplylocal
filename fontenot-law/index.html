Meet the Firm

A Lake Charles Law Firm Built Around You

Divorce is painful. Custody battles are exhausting. A DUI arrest can upend your entire life. When you're facing something like this, you don't need more confusion — you need a Lake Charles law firm that listens, tells you the truth, and fights hard for what matters to you and your family.

Devin Fontenot has been serving families in Lake Charles and Southwest Louisiana for over 10 years. As a lifelong Lake Charles resident — a graduate of Barbe High School and McNeese State University — he understands this community because he's part of it. He earned his law degree from Southern University Law Center, graduating cum laude in 2015, and opened his own firm in 2020 to build the kind of practice he believed clients deserved: one built on honesty, clear communication, and real results.

Before starting his firm, Devin represented juveniles as a public defender in Calcasieu Parish. Today, he gives back as the attorney for Oasis: A Safe Haven, a domestic abuse treatment center serving survivors across Southwest Louisiana. His role isn't to be the hero of your story — it's to guide you through one of the hardest seasons of your life and come out the other side with clarity and confidence.

Frequently Asked Questions

What areas of law does Devin Fontenot practice in Lake Charles?
Devin Fontenot practices family law, criminal defense, and personal injury in Lake Charles, Louisiana. His primary focus is family law — including divorce, child custody, child support, and adoption — serving Calcasieu Parish and Southwest Louisiana.
How long does a divorce take in Louisiana?
An uncontested divorce in Louisiana requires a minimum waiting period of 180 days if you have minor children, or 365 days without. A contested divorce that requires a trial can take 12 to 24 months or longer. See our divorce attorney page for a full breakdown of the process.
Does Devin Fontenot handle DUI cases in Lake Charles?
Yes. Devin handles DUI defense throughout Calcasieu Parish. After a DUI arrest in Louisiana, you have only 15 days to request a hearing to protect your driver's license. If you've been arrested, call (337) 508-2627 immediately.
Can Devin Fontenot help with stepparent or intrafamily adoption in Louisiana?
Yes — adoption is one of Devin's specialties. His firm handles stepparent and intrafamily adoptions throughout Southwest Louisiana, including cases where the biological parent's rights must be legally terminated.
