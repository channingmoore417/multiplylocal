What Is the Google Map Pack?

When someone searches "plumber near me" or "HVAC repair Lake Charles," Google shows a box at the top of the results with a small map and three local business listings. That box is called the map pack — sometimes called the local 3-pack or the local pack.

It displays the business name, star rating, number of reviews, address, hours, and a click-to-call button. It appears above the traditional blue-link organic results and often above paid ads on mobile. For local service businesses, this is where the majority of clicks and calls happen.

Getting into the map pack means you show up at the exact moment someone in your area is looking for the service you provide — with your phone number right there. That's why ranking here matters more than almost anything else in local marketing.

Key Stat

The map pack gets 44% of all clicks on local search results pages. If you're not in those three spots, nearly half of all potential customers will never see your business.

The 4 Ranking Factors That Control the Map Pack

1

Google Business Profile Optimization

Your GBP is the single most important factor for map pack rankings. Every field matters — primary and secondary categories, services, business description, photos, posts, Q&A, and attributes. An incomplete profile tells Google you're not serious. A fully optimized one signals relevance and legitimacy.

2

Review Velocity & Quality

Google doesn't just count your total reviews — it pays attention to how frequently new reviews come in and what they say. A business getting 5 new reviews a month with keyword-rich text will outrank a competitor sitting on 100 old reviews. Velocity and relevance beat volume alone.

3

NAP Consistency & Citations

NAP stands for Name, Address, Phone number. Google cross-references your business information across hundreds of directories — Yelp, Apple Maps, Angi, BBB, and more. If your name is slightly different or your phone number is outdated on even a few of these, Google's confidence in your listing drops and so does your ranking.

4

On-Page Local SEO Signals

Your website reinforces your map pack ranking. Google looks at your title tags, H1s, meta descriptions, local schema markup, and whether your content mentions the services and cities you serve. A website that clearly tells Google "this is a plumber in Lake Charles" strengthens your GBP's authority.

The 90-Day Map Pack Strategy

Ranking in the map pack doesn't require a massive budget or years of waiting. With focused effort over 90 days, most local service businesses can go from invisible to competitive — and often into the top 3. Here's the month-by-month breakdown we use with our clients.

Month 1: GBP Optimization + Citation Building

The first 30 days are about building the foundation. Claim or verify your Google Business Profile if you haven't already. Then optimize every field: choose the most specific primary category, add all relevant secondary categories, write a keyword-rich business description, upload at least 20 high-quality photos, fill out every service you offer, and set accurate hours.

At the same time, start building citations across the top 50+ directories. This means submitting your exact business name, address, and phone number to Yelp, Apple Maps, Bing Places, Angi, HomeAdvisor, BBB, your local Chamber of Commerce, and industry-specific directories. Consistency is everything — every listing must match exactly.

Month 2: Reviews + Content

With your foundation in place, month two shifts to building social proof and content signals. Set up a review generation system — after every completed job, send a short text with a direct link to your Google review page. Aim for 8–12 new reviews per month. Coach happy customers to mention the specific service and city in their review when it feels natural.

On the content side, start publishing GBP posts weekly (project photos, tips, seasonal offers) and create or improve your website's service and location pages. Each service page should target a specific keyword — "drain cleaning Lake Charles" or "AC repair Sulphur LA" — with proper H1 tags, meta descriptions, and local schema markup.

Month 3: Local Links + Optimization

By month three, you have a strong profile, growing reviews, and solid citations. Now it's time to build local authority. Reach out to local blogs, news sites, suppliers, and partner businesses for backlinks. Sponsor a local event or youth sports team and get listed on their website. Join your local Chamber of Commerce if you haven't already — the .org backlink carries weight.

Review your GBP Insights data to see which searches are driving views and calls. Double down on what's working. Respond to every review — positive and negative. Update your GBP photos monthly. Check your citations for accuracy and fix any that have drifted. This ongoing optimization is what separates businesses that hold their ranking from those that spike and drop.

Pro Tip

Set a weekly 30-minute "map pack maintenance" block on your calendar. Use it to post to your GBP, respond to reviews, and check your listing for accuracy. Small consistent actions compound faster than one-time fixes.
