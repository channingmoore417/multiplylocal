Give Us Access to Your Domain

We need to connect your domain to your new website. Select your domain registrar below for specific instructions. If you're not sure who your registrar is, check the email you used to originally purchase your domain — or just send us the login and we'll take care of it.

We're looking for the ability to update your DNS records or add us as a user with domain management access.

  1. Go to domains.google.com (or domains.squarespace.com) and sign in.
  2. Click on your domain name.
  3. Select DNS from the left menu.
  4. Under Custom Records, you can share your login credentials with us securely, or:
  5. Go to Registration settings → Domain permissions and invite channing@multiplylocal.com as an editor.
💡 Note: Google Domains was acquired by Squarespace in 2023. Your domain may have been migrated — log in at squarespace.com to check.
  1. Sign in at godaddy.com and go to your Account Settings.
  2. Click Delegate Access from the left menu.
  3. Click Invite to Access.
  4. Enter channing@multiplylocal.com and select Products & Domains access level.
  5. Click Invite — we'll accept the invite from our end.
💡 Alternatively, you can share your DNS login credentials with us directly if delegated access isn't available on your plan.
  1. Log into namecheap.com and go to the Domain List in your dashboard.
  2. Click Manage next to your domain.
  3. Select the Advanced DNS tab.
  4. From here, share your Namecheap login credentials with us securely OR:
  5. Go to Account → Sharing & Access to grant domain-level access to channing@multiplylocal.com.
💡 Most Namecheap accounts don't have delegation by default — the easiest path is sharing credentials or transferring DNS management to Cloudflare (we can assist).
  1. Log into cloudflare.com and select your account.
  2. Go to Manage Account → Members.
  3. Click Invite Member and enter channing@multiplylocal.com.
  4. Set the role to Administrator or DNS Manager.
  5. Click Send Invite — we'll accept from our side.
