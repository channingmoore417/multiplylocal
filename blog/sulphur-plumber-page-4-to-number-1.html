From Page 4 to #1: How a Sulphur Plumber Tripled His Inbound Calls

Written by Channing Moore
Published February 28, 2026
Read time 10 min
Category Case Study
3x
increase in inbound calls within 90 days

Late last year, a plumber in Sulphur, LA reached out to us with a problem we hear all the time: "I do great work, but nobody can find me online." He was buried on page 4 of Google, had no Google Business Profile to speak of, and was watching competitors with half his skill level book every job in town. Here's exactly what we did — and how it tripled his inbound calls in 90 days.

The Starting Point: Great Work, Zero Online Presence

When this plumber came to us, the situation was rough. He'd been in business for over a decade, had a loyal base of repeat customers, and did the kind of work that earned referrals — but his digital presence told a completely different story.

His website was a single-page template from 2018 with no local SEO, no service pages, and no schema markup. His Google Business Profile was claimed but barely filled out — no categories beyond "Plumber," no services listed, no photos, and no posts. He had 3 Google reviews, the most recent from over a year ago. And when you searched "plumber Sulphur LA" or "plumber near me" from anywhere in the area, he was nowhere to be found — page 4 at best.

Meanwhile, his top three competitors had 40–80 reviews each, fully optimized GBPs, and websites with dedicated service and location pages. They weren't better plumbers. They were just better at showing Google they existed.

The Reality

He was losing 15-20 jobs a month to competitors with worse work but better online presence.

What We Did: The Full Buildout

We didn't do one thing. We built the entire local marketing foundation from scratch — the same system we deploy for every home service client. Here's the breakdown:

1

New Custom Website with Local SEO

We built a fast, mobile-first website with dedicated service pages (drain cleaning, water heater repair, emergency plumbing, etc.), each targeting Sulphur, Lake Charles, and surrounding areas. Every page had proper H1 tags, meta titles, local schema markup, and city-specific content. The site was designed to convert — click-to-call buttons, a quote form above the fold, and trust signals throughout.

2

Google Business Profile — Complete Overhaul

We rebuilt his GBP from the ground up: added every relevant service category, wrote a keyword-rich business description, uploaded 25+ photos of real job sites, set accurate service areas, and added a complete list of services with descriptions. This alone started sending stronger signals to Google within the first two weeks.

3

Citation Building Across 50+ Directories

We submitted his business to over 50 directories — Yelp, Apple Maps, Angi, HomeAdvisor, BBB, Nextdoor, Yellow Pages, and dozens of industry-specific and local directories. Every listing had consistent NAP (Name, Address, Phone) data. This builds the trust signals Google uses to validate a business's legitimacy and location.

4

Automated Review Generation System

We set up an automated system that sends a text message to every customer after a completed job with a direct link to leave a Google review. The message is short, personal, and frictionless — one tap to leave a review. No more hoping customers remember to leave feedback on their own.

5

Weekly GBP Posts + Photo Updates

Every week, we published a Google Business Profile post — seasonal tips, completed job highlights, special offers — along with fresh photos. This signals to Google that the business is active, engaged, and relevant. Most competitors post once and forget about it.

The Results: Month by Month

We tracked everything from day one. Here's what the progression looked like:

Month 1 — Foundation

The new website went live in week one. Citations started indexing by week two. The GBP overhaul was completed on day three. By the end of month one, he had moved from page 4 to page 2 for "plumber Sulphur LA" and related terms. The review system generated 8 new five-star reviews — more than he'd gotten in the previous two years combined. Website traffic increased 74% compared to his old site's baseline.

Month 2 — Momentum

Citations were fully indexed. The GBP was gaining traction from consistent posting and fresh reviews. He hit page 1 for his primary keyword and started appearing in the Google Map Pack for several secondary terms like "emergency plumber Sulphur" and "drain cleaning Lake Charles." He gained 12 new reviews this month, bringing his total to 23. Inbound calls increased noticeably — his phone was ringing from people who found him on Google for the first time.

Month 3 — Dominance

By the end of month three, he was in the #1 Map Pack position for "plumber Sulphur LA" and top 3 for a dozen related terms. He gained 22 new reviews in month three alone, bringing his total to 45 — putting him ahead of two of his three main competitors. His inbound calls had tripled compared to where he was before we started. He went from getting 2–3 calls a week from Google to 8–10. He had to bring on a helper to keep up with demand.

The Takeaway

This case study isn't about one magic trick. There was no single thing that moved the needle — it was the combination of a proper website, a fully optimized GBP, consistent citations, a review system, and ongoing content. Each piece reinforced the others.

If you're a home service business in Southwest Louisiana and you're not showing up on Google, the odds are good that you're in the same position this plumber was: doing excellent work, but giving Google zero reason to recommend you over the competition.

The good news? This is fixable. And it doesn't take years — it takes a system, consistency, and 90 days of focused execution.
