The Google Business Profile Checklist for Home Service Companies

Your Google Business Profile is the single most powerful free marketing tool available to home service companies — and most businesses are leaving 80% of it blank. It's the first thing customers see, the main way Google decides who shows up in the map pack, and the fastest lever you can pull to start getting more calls. This is the checklist you need to fill out every field that actually matters.

Why Your Google Business Profile Matters More Than Your Website

When someone searches "plumber near me" or "AC repair" on their phone, they don't see websites first — they see the Google Map Pack. Three businesses with reviews, hours, phone numbers, and photos. That map pack is powered entirely by your Google Business Profile. If yours is incomplete, outdated, or thin on content, you're handing that spot to a competitor.

Your GBP is also where most people take action. They tap to call, tap for directions, or read your reviews — all without ever visiting your website. For many home service companies, the GBP generates more leads than the website itself. Treating it as an afterthought is leaving money on the table.

The 7-Step GBP Checklist

1

Business Name & Categories

Your business name should match your real-world branding exactly — don't stuff keywords into it. Choose a primary category that matches your core service (e.g., "Plumber" not "Home Improvement"), then add every relevant secondary category Google offers. Categories are one of the strongest ranking signals in local search.

2

Complete Service List

Google lets you add individual services under each category — and most businesses skip this entirely. Add every service you offer with a brief description and price range if applicable. This helps Google match your profile to specific searches like "tankless water heater installation" instead of just "plumber."

3

Business Description

You get 750 characters. Use them. Lead with what you do and where you do it. Mention your service area, your specialties, and what makes you different. Don't keyword-stuff — write for humans first. But make sure your primary service and city appear naturally in the first two sentences.

4

Photos — At Least 10

Businesses with more than 10 photos get 35% more clicks to their website than those without. Upload photos of your team, your trucks, completed jobs, before-and-afters, and your office or shop. Real photos outperform stock images every time. Google also uses photo metadata and engagement signals as a ranking factor.

5

Google Posts — Weekly

Google Posts are short updates that appear directly on your profile — think of them as mini social media posts. Publish one every week. Share a completed project, a seasonal tip, a promotion, or a customer testimonial. Posts signal to Google that your business is active, and they give searchers more reasons to choose you.

6

Q&A Section

Most businesses don't realize anyone can ask (and answer) questions on their GBP. Take control by pre-populating your Q&A with the questions customers ask you most — pricing, service areas, availability, licensing. Then answer them yourself. This builds trust and gives Google more content to index.

7

Reviews & Responses

Reviews are the social proof engine of your GBP. Build a system to ask every customer for a review after each job — a simple text with a direct link works best. But just as important: respond to every single review, positive or negative. Google has confirmed that responding to reviews improves your local ranking.

First Impressions Matter

Your GBP is often the first impression a potential customer has of your business. 64% of people use Google Business Profile to find contact info for a local business. If your profile looks empty or outdated, they're calling someone else.

The Fields Most Businesses Skip (That Actually Matter)

Beyond the basics, Google has added a number of profile fields that most businesses ignore entirely — and they're leaving ranking signals on the table.

Attributes

Google lets you mark attributes like "veteran-owned," "women-owned," "free estimates," "emergency service available," and many more. These show up as badges on your profile and can influence both click-through rates and search matching. Go through every attribute available for your category and check every one that applies.

Products

Even if you're a service business, you can use the Products section to showcase your service packages or key offerings with images and descriptions. Think of it as a mini storefront on your profile. Businesses that use Products see more engagement and longer profile views.

Service Areas

If you travel to customers (like most home service businesses), define your service areas explicitly. You can list up to 20 cities or regions. This tells Google where you're willing to work and helps you show up in searches from those areas — even if your physical address is elsewhere.

How Often Should You Update Your GBP?

A set-it-and-forget-it profile is a dead profile. Google rewards freshness, and your competitors who post regularly will outrank you over time. Here's the cadence that works:

Weekly: Google Posts

Publish at least one Google Post per week. Rotate between job photos, tips, offers, and customer wins. Each post stays visible for about 7 days, so consistency matters. This is the single easiest way to signal to Google that your business is active and engaged.

Monthly: Photo Updates

Add 3–5 new photos every month. Job site photos, finished work, team shots — anything real. Profiles with recent photos get more views and more direction requests. It also pushes your profile ahead of competitors who uploaded 4 photos in 2022 and never touched it again.

Quarterly: Full Profile Audit

Every 90 days, go through your entire profile. Update your services if you've added new ones. Refresh your business description. Check your hours for seasonal changes. Review and respond to any unanswered questions in Q&A. A quarterly audit ensures nothing falls through the cracks.
